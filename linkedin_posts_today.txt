1. COLLABORATIVE ARTICLE
==================================================
A founder running a subcontractor heavy legal staffing agency at $3 million in revenue should be thriving. Instead, they feel broke every single month.

The bank balance looks high after a large client deposit for a corporate staffing project. This creates a false sense of security. The founder spends based on the total balance, ignoring that most of that money belongs to someone else.

They pay subcontractors on net 30 terms, which temporarily inflates the cash on hand. By the time the invoice comes due for a group of senior associates or specialized IP consultants, the funds are gone. The founder spends the rest of the month stressing over payroll while the accounting software shows a profit.

This is the common gap between accounting and cash allocation. Accounting tells you if the business is profitable on paper. Cash allocation tells you if you can actually pay your bills tomorrow. Profit is a calculation. Cash is a reality.

The breakthrough happens when the founder stops treating the main business account as a single bucket. They move from a single account to a segmented system. Every single deposit is split immediately upon receipt.

Forty percent goes to a subcontractor account. Twenty percent goes to a tax reserve. Twenty percent covers operating expenses. The remaining twenty percent is actual profit.

When the money for the subcontractors is tucked away in a separate account, it is no longer available for a new office lease or a luxury software subscription. The founder sees exactly what they own and what they owe.

This shift removes the mental load of calculating liabilities in their head. They no longer guess if they can afford a new hire. They check the OpEx account. If the money is there, the hire is sustainable.

Law firms and staffing agencies that manage high volume contractor spends often fail because they confuse deposits with earnings. Dividing funds at the point of entry ensures the business stays solvent during lean months.

==================================================
Law firms cannot risk public errors caused by AI making up facts.

One wrong citation in a court filing or a client report creates a reputation crisis.

Prevent this by using a two agent audit loop. 

First, have one AI draft the report. Then, feed that draft and the original source documents into a second AI with a specific instruction: "Identify every claim in the draft that is not explicitly supported by the source text and flag it as an error."

This forces the AI to act as a critic. It catches false claims before a partner sees them.
